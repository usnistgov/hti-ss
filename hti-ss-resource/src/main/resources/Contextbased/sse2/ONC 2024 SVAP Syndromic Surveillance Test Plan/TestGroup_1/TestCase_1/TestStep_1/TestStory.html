Description

A mother brings her 6-month old male infant to NIST Hospital`s Urgent Care on February 20, 2025, at 8:30 AM. A clerical assistant registers the patient. She records the patient`s name, date of birth, race, ethnicity, residence, insurance information (PlanID123 from MyInsuranceCompany), and health history. The clerical assistant also records the patient's chief complaint in free-text is, "Fever, cough, and earache." NIST Hospital's Urgent Care is an outpatient facility operated by NIST Hospital. 

NIST Hospital routinely sends electronic syndromic surveillance data to the City Health Department (NIST PH SS FAC 1) in accordance  with a city regulation for all of it's facilities within that municipal jurisdiction. At 10:00 AM, NIST Hospital`s electronic health record module for syndromic surveillance data assembles and transmits a Registration ADT message about this patient encounter on behalf of the Urgent Care facility as part of an automated process that transmits new or updated content every 15 minutes. The patient name and birthdate are de-identified in the submission, so no date of birth is included, and the name is represented with name type 'S' to indicate that the name is empty for anonymity.

The dates within the message and across the messages in this test case will be subject to visual inspection to verify data consistencies and the ability to conform to the requirement to send this message within 12 hours of the event triggering this message.



Comments

Dates and times are provided in this test scenario to illustrate the sequence of clinical and messaging events. Since the exact dates and times are not reproducible when modeling the test scenario with HIT, only date and time format will be validated within tester submitted test data.

Test Objectives

This test step examines an HIT Module's ability to create an ADT^A04 Registration message within HL7 Version 2.5.1 Implementation Guide: Syndromic Surveillance, Release 1 - US Realm Standard for Trial Use July 2019, conformance requirements.

The following test objectives and variants are supported by this test case:

  • Setting is Urgent Care.
  • Chief complaint is submitted as free-text.
  • Patient name is de-identified in the submissions: Per ASTP/ONC, for certification testing HIT developers must demonstrate that their system supports the Patient Name and de-identified data in PID-5 (this policy is conformant to the named standard HL7 Version 2.5.1 Implementation Guide: Syndromic Surveillance, Release 1 - US Realm Standard for Trial Use July 2019).The patient name presented in a de-identified manner is required for this Test Step.
  • Pediatric case where age is measured in months.
  • Multiple Races
  • Insurance is known

Evaluation Criteria

Message Validation Report


Notes

Although the other units of measure for patient age are acceptable in general (and the Context-free validation accepts any of the valid units of measure for age), this Scenario specifies that "mo" for months be used in this message for the Context-based validation.

Messages in this test case will be used in a Manual Test Step under functional requirement testing.

The MSH-4 value is agreed upon by the sender and receiver.
