Test Case SS-UC-1_UC_Visit_Influenza_Child

Description

Syndromic surveillance is public health surveillance that emphasizes the use of near "real-time" health data and statistical tools for disease or hazardous event detection, situation awareness for mass gatherings and public health emergencies, and ad hoc and population health trend analyses. For syndromic surveillance purposes, required data on all clinical encounters must be sent to the jurisdictional public health agency within 12 hours of any given encounter, and in accordance with local or state laws and practices.
The Influenza Child Test Case provides an example of a clinical encounter that could take place in an urgent care setting. This test case also provides an example where the facility sending the syndromic surveillance message is different than the facility where the patient received care. In this test case, an infant is seen at an urgent care center for fever, cough, and earache. Diagnostic tests show that he has influenza and stenosis of the external ear canal. He is discharged to home with treatment instructions and a prescription. Syndromic surveillance data about the visit are transmitted to the local health department.


This test case involves 3 steps:

  • a registration message (ADT^A04),
  • followed by an update message (ADT^A08),
  • followed by a discharge message (ADT^A03).


Messages in this test case will be used in a Manual Test Step under functional requirement testing for validating the HIT capability of populating Visit ID (PV1-19.1) and Visit Time (PV1-44.1) each with the same value for all messages included in this Test Case.

Description

A mother brings her 6-month old male infant to NIST Hospital`s Urgent Care on February 20, 2025, at 8:30 AM. A clerical assistant registers the patient. She records the patient`s name, date of birth, race, ethnicity, residence, insurance information (PlanID123 from NIST INSURANCE 1), and health history. The clerical assistant also records the patient's chief complaint in free-text is, "Fever, cough, and earache." NIST Hospital's Urgent Care is an outpatient facility operated by NIST Hospital. 

NIST Hospital routinely sends electronic syndromic surveillance data to the City Health Department (NIST PH SS FAC 1) in accordance  with a city regulation for all of it's facilities within that municipal jurisdiction. At 10:00 AM, NIST Hospital`s electronic health record module for syndromic surveillance data assembles and transmits a Registration ADT message about this patient encounter on behalf of the Urgent Care facility as part of an automated process that transmits new or updated content every 15 minutes. The patient name and birthdate are de-identified in the submission, so no date of birth is included, and the name is represented with name type 'S' to indicate that the name is empty for anonymity.

The dates within the message and across the messages in this test case will be subject to visual inspection to verify data consistencies and the ability to conform to the requirement to send this message within 12 hours of the event triggering this message.
