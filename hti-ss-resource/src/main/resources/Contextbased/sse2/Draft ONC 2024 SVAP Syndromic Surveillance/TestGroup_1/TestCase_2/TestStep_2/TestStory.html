Description

At 12:30 PM, a nurse weighs the patient and measures his height and weight, which determines the patient BMI, and at 1pm. sets up an IV. This information is charted in the patient’s record to support the physician in ordering any medications. At 5:00 PM, the physician assigns the patient the reason for visit of ‘Noninfective gastroenteritis and colitis, unspecified’  and documents the information in the patient`s electronic medical record. The provider sends a stool sample to the lab for confirmation, and documents Home subject to flooding (finding) (SNOMED-CT 224259003)  and a working diagnosis of Gastroenteritis (disorder) (SNOMED-CT 25374005) in the patient record, and the patient is discharged home. The provider also documents admitting diagnoses of Dehydration (disorder) (SNOMED-CT 34095006),  and Nausea and vomiting (disorder) (SNOMED-CT 16932000).

NIST Urgent Care is an independently operated outpatient facility that routinely sends electronic syndromic surveillance data to the State Department of Public Health (NIST PH SS FAC 2) in accordance with state regulation. The state regulations permit sending identifiable patient information for the purposes of syndromic surveillance. in accordance with state regulation. At 6:00 PM on October 20, 2024, the facility`s electronic health record module for syndromic surveillance data assembles and transmits a Discharge ADT message about this patient encounter to the state. The "full, legal patient name" and birth date is included due to state required reporting of all patients, which is not always the case across all jurisdictions.  


Comments

Dates and times are provided in this test scenario to illustrate the sequence of clinical and messaging events. Since the exact dates and times are not reproducible when modeling the test scenario with HIT, only date and time format will be validated within tester submitted test data. The dates within the message and across the messages in this test case will be subject to visual inspection to verify data consistencies and the ability to conform to the requirement to send this message within 12 hours of the event triggering this message.
