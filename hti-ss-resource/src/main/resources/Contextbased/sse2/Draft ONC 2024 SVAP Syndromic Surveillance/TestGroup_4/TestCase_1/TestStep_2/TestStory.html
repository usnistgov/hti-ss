Description

The pediatrician suspects measles and orders a Real-time RT-PCR to confirm, and provides instruction for home isolation to prevent potential spread.

A nurse weighs the patient, measures his length, and charts the data in the patient’s record at 8:35. This information may be used by the physician if ordering the take-home prescription.  At 8:55 AM, the physician assigns the patient the working diagnoses of measles: (ICD-10 CM diagnosis code of Measles with intestinal complication B05.4), Conjunctivitis (disorder) SNOMED-CT 9826008), Fever, unspecified (SNOMED-CT 426000000), and Hacking cough (finding)  (SNOMED-CT 59994004)  are documented as final in the patient`s electronic medical record. Suspecting Measles, the provider takes a nasal swab to send for Real-time RT-PCR. The provider recommends isolation at home, providing education to the parents.  Education about isolation for infection control (procedure) (SNOMED-CT 737612005). The visit is closed at 9:05 am after the nurse collects the sample to send to the lab.  

NIST Primary Care is an outpatient facility that routinely sends electronic syndromic surveillance data to the City Health Department (NIST PH SS FAC 1) in accordance with a city regulation. At 6:00 PM, the facility`s electronic health record module for syndromic surveillance data assembles and transmits a Discharge ADT message with height and weight information, as well as the working and final diagnoses for this patient encounter. The patient name and birthdate are de-identified in the submission, so no date of birth is included, and the name is represented with name type 'S' to indicate that the name is empty for anonymity.













Comments

The dates and times in this test case illustrate the sequence of clinical and messaging events. Since the exact dates and times are not reproducible when modeling the test case with EHR technology, only date and time format will be validated within tester submitted test data.

The dates within the message and across the messages in this test case will be subject to visual inspection to verify data consistencies and the ability to conform to the requirement to send this message within 12 hours of the event triggering this message.

A04-Registration message is sent before the A03-Discharge message.


Post-Condition
